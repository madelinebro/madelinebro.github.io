Resume and Education

Relevant coursework: Introduction to Electrical Engineering: This foundational course covers the fundamental principles of electrical engineering, including circuit analysis, basic electronics, and electrical systems. Students learn about Ohm's Law, Kirchhoff's Laws, and basic circuit analysis techniques. Calculus I & II: Calculus courses are essential for understanding advanced mathematical concepts used in electrical engineering, such as differential equations, integrals, and limits. These courses provide the mathematical foundation necessary for analyzing circuits and systems. Physics I & II (with Calculus): Physics courses introduce students to fundamental principles of classical mechanics, electromagnetism, and thermodynamics. Physics with calculus focuses on applying mathematical concepts to solve physics problems, providing a basis for understanding electrical phenomena. Digital Logic Design: This course introduces students to the principles of digital logic and the design of digital systems. Topics include Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational and sequential circuits, and finite state machines. Students gain hands-on experience designing and implementing digital circuits. Analog Electronics: Analog electronics courses cover the theory and design of analog circuits, including amplifiers, operational amplifiers, filters, and feedback systems. Students learn about transistor operation, biasing techniques, and small-signal analysis. Linear Algebra: Linear algebra is essential for understanding complex circuit analysis, control systems, and signal processing. Topics include vectors, matrices, systems of linear equations, eigenvalues, and eigenvectors. Engineering Electromagnetics: This course explores the fundamental principles of electromagnetism, including Maxwell's equations, electromagnetic waves, transmission lines, and waveguides. Students learn how electromagnetic theory applies to the design of antennas, RF circuits, and communication systems. Circuit Analysis: Building upon introductory circuit theory, this course delves deeper into the analysis of electrical circuits. Topics include AC circuit analysis, network theorems, frequency response, and impedance matching. Students learn to analyze complex circuits using techniques such as nodal analysis and mesh analysis.
